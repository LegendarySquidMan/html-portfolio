About Me

For more than eight centuries, the gentleman known only as Mr. Brian has occupied the narrow space between visionary entrepreneur and civilization’s most elegantly dressed catastrophe. Born sometime during an unfortunate theological misunderstanding beneath the oldest circles of the infernal abyss, Mr. Brian emerged into mortal society with an unnatural aptitude for mathematics, persuasion, and market manipulation. Historians have quietly attributed several industrial revolutions to his “consulting work”, though official records tend to disappear shortly after auditors begin asking questions. By the late Renaissance, he had already financed alchemists, destabilized monarchies through commodity speculation, and patented three inventions centuries before humanity was emotionally prepared for them. Today, he is regarded as a celebrated technologist, private investor, and experimental scientist whose laboratories produce innovations so advanced they occasionally violate local laws.

Despite rumors of necromantic board meetings and an unsettling tendency to never appear in photographs prior to 1861, Mr. Brian remains exceptionally charming company. Colleagues describe him as incisively intelligent, impeccably mannered, and “surprisingly approachable for someone who may have personally negotiated with Lucifer”. Between advising governments, acquiring distressed empires, and conducting morally ambiguous research beneath undisclosed volcanic properties, Mr. Brian maintains a disciplined devotion to penny-farthing bicycling through fog-covered city streets at unreasonable hours. His fortune is never discussed directly, though financial markets tend to fluctuate whenever he purchases a new waistcoat. Those fortunate enough to receive an invitation to his candlelit estate often leave inspired, enriched, and very slightly concerned about the future of humanity.
